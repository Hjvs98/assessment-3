Orem, Utah

"Family City USA"

Orem is a city in Utah County, Utah, United States, in the northern part of the state. It is adjacent to Provo, Lindon, and Vineyard and is approximately 45 miles (72 km) south of Salt Lake City. Orem is one of the principal cities of the Provo-Orem, Utah Metropolitan Statistical Area, which includes all of Utah and Juab counties. The 2020 population was 98,129,[1] while the 2010 population was 88,328[5] making it the fifth-largest city in Utah. Utah Valley University is located in Orem.

Parks

City Center Park

Location: Center Street & 300 E

City Center Park is one of the most versatile parks in the city. It is home to the annual Summerfest celebration as well as The All-Together (all-abilities) Playground. Concerts and other cultural performances are held at the City Center Stage within the park. This is definitely one of the gems of the Orem parks system.

Amenities:

  • 23.1 Acres

  • Six Baseball Fields

  • Baseball Snack Shack

  • All-Together Playground

  • Two Restrooms

  • Two Large Pavilions with BBQ Braziers (Seats 120 & 300 people)

  • Walking Path - 1.34 miles

  • Outdoor Stage

Mount Timpanogos Park

Location: US-189, Orem UT

Nestled in the beauty of Provo Canyon, Mt. Timpanogos Park is home to the world’s largest storytelling festival, the Timpanogos Storytelling Festival. When the festival isn’t in town, the park is great for hiking, walking, and picnicking with friends and family.

Amenities:

  • 12 Acres

  • Restrooms

  • Hosting center for weddings, receptions, parties, etc.

  • Connects to several hiking trails

  • 1 large pavilion (Seats 120)

  • 6 small pavilions (Seats 24-40)

  • Walking Trail - 1.27 miles

Nielson Grove Park

Location: 1931 S. Sandhill Road

Nielsen’s Grove is the oldest park in Utah. The park was started in 1880 by Danish immigrant Jorgen Nielson. The park is the only park with a water feature and contains many design elements found in European castle gardens.

Amenities:

  • 21 Acres

  • Pond

  • Fountain

  • Large Swings

  • Playground

  • Restrooms

  • Garden area with 24,000 tulips in the Spring

  • Historic Structures

  • 1 large pavilion with BBQ Braziers – (Seats 120)

  • 11 small pavilions (Seats 8)

  • Walking Path – 2 miles
